📚 Conceptual Foundation

IV Rank contextualises current implied volatility against its 52-week range. It answers: where does today's IV sit between the annual high and low?

IV Rank = (Current IV − 52wk Low) / (52wk High − 52wk Low) × 100

Key caveat: One crisis spike can suppress readings for an entire year. Always confirm with IV Percentile.

IV Percentile shows what percentage of days over the past year had lower IV than today. More robust than IV Rank — considers the full distribution.

IV Percentile = (Days with IV < Current IV) / Total Days × 100

Volatility exhibits strong mean-reverting behaviour. High IV tends to fall, low IV tends to rise. The mean reversion half-life is approximately 2–4 weeks — giving premium sellers a systematic edge. On average, IV exceeds HV by 2–3 percentage points (the "volatility risk premium").

India VIX spikes before elections, RBI policy, and Union Budget — then collapses post-event, creating ideal premium-selling setups.
